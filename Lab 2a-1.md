Lab 2a-1: Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) Analysis – Printer Comparison

Printer Models Chosen

    Printer A: Canon Pixma (Entry-level Inkjet Color Printer)

    Printer B: Brother HL-1210W (Office-class Mono Laser Printer)

Cost Assumptions Documented

    Time period: 5 Years

    Total pages to be printed: 180,000 pages over 5 years (based on ~3,000 pages/month).

    Electricity cost: $0.3478 per kWh (inclusive of 9% GST).

    Paper cost: $8.50 per ream (500 sheets).

    Both printers are assumed to last the full 5-year lifecycle without requiring replacement.

List of Expense Items

Fixed Costs:

    Initial printer purchase price

    Electricity (fixed yearly power consumption)

Variable Costs (Consumables):

    Paper

    Ink Cartridges (Canon)

    Toner and Drum Unit (Brother)

Unit Costs and Yields Sourced

    Canon Pixma Printer: $84.60

    Brother HL-1210W Printer: $117.90

    Canon HP 68 Black Ink: $49.50 per cartridge (160 pages yield)

    Canon HP 68 Tri-Color Ink: $49.20 per cartridge (120 pages yield)

    Brother TN-1000 Toner: $62.00 per unit (1,000 pages yield)

    Brother DR-1000 Drum Unit: $42.00 per unit (10,000 pages yield)

    Paper: $8.50 per 500-sheet ream

    Electricity: $0.3478 / kWh

Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) Calculations

Paper Cost:
180,000 total pages ÷ 500 pages per ream = 360 reams required.
360 reams × $8.50 per ream = $3,060.00 total paper cost over 5 years.

Electricity Cost:
We will estimate an average of $8.65 per year for both printers.
$8.65 × 5 years = $43.25 total electricity cost.

Canon Pixma TCO:

    Fixed Printer Cost: $84.60

    Electricity: $43.25

    Black Ink Cartridges Required: 180,000 pages ÷ 160 pages/cartridge = 1,125 cartridges. Cost: 1,125 × $49.50 = $55,687.50

    Tri-Color Ink Cartridges Required: 180,000 pages ÷ 120 pages/cartridge = 1,500 cartridges. Cost: 1,500 × $49.20 = $73,800.00

    Canon Consumables Subtotal: $129,487.50

    Canon TCO (Excluding Paper): $84.60 + $43.25 + $129,487.50 = $129,615.35

    Canon Final TCO (Including Paper): $129,615.35 + $3,060.00 = $132,675.35

Brother HL-1210W TCO:

    Fixed Printer Cost: $117.90

    Electricity: $43.25

    Toner Cartridges Required: 180,000 pages ÷ 1,000 pages/toner = 180 toners. Cost: 180 × $62.00 = $11,160.00

    Drum Units Required: 180,000 pages ÷ 10,000 pages/drum = 18 drums. Cost: 18 × $42.00 = $756.00

    Brother Consumables Subtotal: $11,916.00

    Brother TCO (Excluding Paper): $117.90 + $43.25 + $11,916.00 = $12,077.15

    Brother Final TCO (Including Paper): $12,077.15 + $3,060.00 = $15,137.15

Cost Per Page

    Canon Pixma: $132,675.35 ÷ 180,000 pages = **$0.737 per page**

    Brother HL-1210W: $15,137.15 ÷ 180,000 pages = **$0.084 per page**

Break-Even Analysis

To find the point at which the Brother printer becomes cheaper than the Canon, we calculate the fixed and variable costs per page.

    Canon Fixed Costs: $84.60 (printer) + $43.25 (electricity) = $127.85

    Canon Variable Cost per page: $49.50 ÷ 160 pages = $0.309 per page

    Brother Fixed Costs: $117.90 (printer) + $43.25 (electricity) + $756.00 (total drum cost) = $917.15

    Brother Variable Cost per page: $62.00 ÷ 1,000 pages = $0.062 per page

Break-even equation:
127.85 + 0.309x = 917.15 + 0.062x
0.247x = 789.30
x = 3,196 pages

Conclusion: If you print less than 3,196 pages, the Canon is the cheaper option. If you print more than 3,196 pages, the Brother is more economical. In a scenario printing 180,000 pages, the Brother is the only logical choice.

Reflection

Which printer has lower TCO and why?
The Brother HL-1210W has a drastically lower TCO ($15,137.15 vs $132,675.35). This enormous difference is driven by the cost of consumables. The Canon inkjet requires over 2,600 cartridges to print 180,000 pages, costing tens of thousands of dollars. The Brother laser, however, requires only 180 toners and 18 drum units, making its long-term operating costs a fraction of the inkjet's.

Would the choice change if only 5 pages/week were printed?
Yes, absolutely. At just 5 pages per week (roughly 260 pages per year), consumables become almost irrelevant. The Canon, with its much lower upfront cost of $84.60, would be the far more reasonable choice. The high upfront cost of the Brother would never be justified at that low volume.

What other non-financial factors could influence printer selection?
Print quality is a major factor—the Canon is a color printer suitable for photos and graphics, while the Brother is a black-and-white printer only. Print speed also matters; the Brother laser will print pages much faster than the Canon inkjet, which is critical in a busy office environment. Additionally, laser printers generally have fewer maintenance issues, as inkjets can dry out and clog if not used regularly.

What cost components are more significant for a large workgroup printer?
For a high-volume workgroup, ink and toner costs are overwhelmingly the most significant expense. In this calculation, ink accounted for over 95% of the Canon's total cost, and consumables accounted for over 70% of the Brother's. While paper is a considerable cost, the efficiency of the printing consumable is the primary driver of TCO.

Estimate the break-even time point.
The break-even point is calculated at exactly 3,196 pages. For a high-volume office printing 180,000 pages over 5 years, this break-even point would be reached within the first 1 to 2 months of operation. After that, every single page printed on the Brother represents a substantial financial saving over the Canon.
